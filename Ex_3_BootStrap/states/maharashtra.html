Festivals in Maharashtra

Ganesh Chaturthi

Description: Ganesh Chaturthi celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha. It is marked by the installation of Ganesha idols, followed by ten days of prayers, feasting, and cultural events.

Date: August/September (varies by year)

Significance: The festival symbolizes the removal of obstacles and the beginning of new ventures with blessings from Lord Ganesha.

Diwali

Description: Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is celebrated by lighting oil lamps, bursting firecrackers, and enjoying festive sweets. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Date: October/November (varies by year)

Significance: Diwali is a time for family celebrations, prayers, and cultural activities.

Makar Sankranti

Description: Makar Sankranti is a harvest festival celebrated by flying kites, preparing sweets like tilgul, and exchanging good wishes.

Date: January 14

Significance: It marks the end of the winter solstice and the beginning of longer days.
