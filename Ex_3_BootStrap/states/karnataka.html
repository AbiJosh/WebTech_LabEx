Festivals in Karnataka

Dussehra

Description: Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is celebrated with great grandeur in Karnataka, especially in Mysore. It marks the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana and the goddess Chamundeshwari over the demon Mahishasura.

Date: September/October (varies by year)

Significance: The festival represents the triumph of good over evil and is known for processions and cultural festivities.

Ugadi

Description: Ugadi is the Kannada New Year, marking the beginning of the Hindu lunar calendar. It is celebrated by decorating homes, offering prayers, and enjoying traditional dishes.

Date: March/April (varies by year)

Significance: Ugadi symbolizes a new start, prosperity, and growth.

Makar Sankranti

Description: Makar Sankranti is a harvest festival celebrating the Sun's transition into Capricorn. It is marked by flying kites, preparing special dishes, and family gatherings.

Date: January 14

Significance: The festival signifies the end of winter and the beginning of longer days.
