Festivals in West Bengal

Durga Puja

Description: Durga Puja is the most significant festival in West Bengal, celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura. It involves grand decorations, processions, cultural performances, and elaborate rituals.

Date: September/October (varies by year)

Significance: The festival symbolizes the triumph of good over evil and is a time for family reunions and celebrations.

Kali Puja

Description: Kali Puja is dedicated to Goddess Kali, the goddess of destruction. The festival is marked by offering prayers, lighting lamps, and organizing cultural events.

Date: October/November (varies by year)

Significance: Kali Puja celebrates the power of Goddess Kali and the destruction of evil forces.

Poila Baisakh

Description: Poila Baisakh marks the Bengali New Year and is celebrated with great enthusiasm. People wear new clothes, visit temples, and enjoy traditional feasts.

Date: April 14/15

Significance: The festival signifies a fresh start to the new year with hopes for prosperity and happiness.
